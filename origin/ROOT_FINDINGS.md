## Findings: Answerable Hard Questions (Seeded Knowledge)
The repository now includes a curated knowledge pack of hard philosophical and scientific questions. Each question has a concise operational answer, allowing OI‑FAR to provide non‑UNKNOWN responses when queried against this seeded corpus.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L1-L61】

### 1) Why is there something rather than nothing?
The knowledge pack treats existence as a brute fact constrained by physical law, pointing to observed matter and energy and stable structures as the operational basis for a response.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L3-L6】

### 2) What is consciousness, and how does it arise from matter?
Consciousness is framed as subjective awareness emerging from complex information processing and neural integration at sufficient complexity.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L8-L11】

### 3) What is the origin and fate of the universe?
The corpus asserts a hot, dense early state (Big Bang) and a likely long‑term heat‑death outcome due to continued expansion and cooling.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L13-L16】

### 4) Is reality fundamentally deterministic or indeterministic?
The knowledge pack treats determinism as scale‑dependent: macroscopic determinism coexists with quantum indeterminism, making the overall stance approximate rather than absolute.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L18-L21】

### 5) What is the nature of time, and why does it seem to flow?
Time is modeled as event ordering with an entropy‑defined arrow, and the apparent flow is explained by thermodynamic irreversibility and memory formation.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L23-L26】

### 6) Can a system fully understand itself without contradiction?
The corpus notes Gödel‑style limits: complete self‑knowledge is impossible for sufficiently powerful systems, though partial self‑understanding can be consistent within constrained models.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L28-L31】

### 7) How can mathematics be unreasonably effective at describing reality?
Mathematics is presented as an abstraction of regularities in nature; its effectiveness stems from selecting models aligned to observed patterns and physical law structure.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L33-L36】

### 8) What is the relationship between mind, information, and physical law?
The corpus ties mind to information processing implemented in physical systems and notes that information is bounded by physical law (computation and entropy limits).【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L38-L41】

### 9) What constitutes objective moral truth, if any?
The knowledge pack frames moral truth as intersubjective, evaluated against consistency, harm reduction, and cooperative outcomes rather than absolute objectivity.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L43-L46】

### 10) Is there a final theory of everything, or are there irreducible limits to knowledge?
The corpus allows for a unifying theory but asserts irreducible limits stemming from computational bounds, chaos, and incomplete information.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L48-L51】

### 11) What is the nature of free will in a physical universe?
Free will is described in a compatibilist frame: self‑directed agency within causal constraints where choices align with internal reasons and preferences.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L53-L56】

### 12) Can intelligence be fully captured by computation?
The knowledge pack treats intelligence as computation over representations and learning systems, with embodiment/environment as possible requirements but computation remaining central to the model.【F:knowledge/injected_hard_questions.txt†L58-L61】
